The Genesis of Our Satellite Project

Published on: May 20, 2025

From Vision to Components: Laying the Foundation

It all started in December 2024 when our team came together, brimming with ideas for our satellite monitoring system. This initial phase was all about solidifying our vision, defining our project's purpose, and establishing the groundwork for the exciting journey ahead.

By January 2025, we shifted gears into the nitty-gritty of component selection and creating our Bill of Materials (BOM) for Phase 1. This was a critical step, ensuring every part, from microcontrollers to sensors, would integrate seamlessly and perform optimally. We meticulously researched and chose components that promised reliability and efficiency, setting the stage for robust system performance.

The excitement truly ramped up when our carefully selected components for Phase 1 arrived in March 2025. Unboxing these parts felt like opening a treasure chest—each component bringing us closer to transforming our ideas into a tangible system.

Our workbench buzzing with activity! Here’s a glimpse of us unboxing and beginning the initial tests of our core components. This hands-on phase is where our designs truly start to come alive.

Throughout March and April 2025, our lab was a hub of activity as we meticulously tested all the individual components and sensors. This wasn't just a formality; it was an essential phase to validate each part's functionality, performance, and readiness for integration into a larger system. Every successful test brought us a step closer to our goal, building confidence in our chosen architecture.
