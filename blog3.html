Designing the Future: PCB, 3D Model, and Final Assembly

Published on: July 1, 2025

From Prototype to Product: The Art of Design and Assembly

With our breadboard prototype proven, May 2025 saw us dive into the sophisticated world of PCB (Printed Circuit Board) designing and 3D model designing. This phase was about transforming our functional prototype into a compact, robust, and aesthetically pleasing satellite unit. We meticulously designed the PCB, considering component placement, signal integrity, and power distribution to ensure optimal performance in a constrained space. Simultaneously, our 3D model took shape, focusing on material selection, thermal management, and a compact form factor that would protect our sensitive electronics.

The brain of our satellite: Our custom-designed Printed Circuit Board, engineered for compactness and efficiency. This is where all the magic happens!

Bringing our vision to life in three dimensions! This detailed 3D model showcases the sleek, compact design of our satellite, ready for its purpose.

From June 2025 to the present, our focus has been dual-pronged. We've been tirelessly conducting communication range testing with advanced encoding and decoding methods for enhanced data security. Ensuring the integrity and confidentiality of our transmitted data is paramount. In parallel, our workbench has been busy with PCB testing and the meticulous assembly of all components onto the fabricated boards. This process requires precision and patience, ensuring every connection is perfect.

Currently, we're in the incredibly exciting final stages of finalizing the structure of the satellite and preparing it for 3D printing. This is where all our hard work on design and integration culminates, as we prepare to see our digital models transform into physical hardware. We're on the cusp of bringing our satellite monitoring system to life, and we can't wait to share the next update!
